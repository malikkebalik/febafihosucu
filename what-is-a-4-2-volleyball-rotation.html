What is a 4 2 volleyball rotation?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, July 27, 2024

Rotations for running a 4-2 offense The 4-2 serve receive is the most basic volleyball offensive system. In a 4-2 system you have 2 setters and only 4 hitters. The two setters play opposite one another on the court. The setter that is on the front row sets for that rotation.Click to see full answer. The two most common offensive systems in volleyball are the 5-1 system, which uses five hitters and one setter for all six rotations, and the 6-2 system, which uses a total of six hitters and two different setters, depending on where they are in the rotation. Also, what is the difference between a 4 2 and 6’2 in volleyball? The basic difference between the 4-2 and the 6-2 is where the setter is in the lineup. In both offenses, you’ve got 2 setters, and they’ll be opposite each other in the lineup (1 and 4, 2 and 5, etc).
