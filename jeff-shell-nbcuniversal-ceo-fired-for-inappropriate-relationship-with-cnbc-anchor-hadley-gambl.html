On Sunday, Comcast announced that NBCUniversal CEO Jeff Shell had been fired due to “an inappropriate relationship” with a CNBC anchor. According to reports, an outside counsel’s probe into a complaint lodged against him uncovered evidence of the alleged affair.

“Today is my last day as CEO of NBCUniversal,” Shell said in a statement released on Sunday 23 April. I regret greatly that I engaged in an inappropriate relationship with a female coworker.

My sincere apologies to my colleagues at Comcast and NBCUniversal, it reads in the statement. Working with them over the past decade has been an honor since they are some of the most skilled professionals in the field.

According to Deadline.com, Hadley Gamble, a news presenter and senior international correspondent, was the woman Shell cheated on her husband with. The publication claims the couple began dating 11 years ago and have been together off and on ever since.

Shell, who is in a committed relationship, has worked for the firm for twenty years.

According to The Wall Street Journal, he oversaw the entertainment division of the media conglomerate before leaving when the company’s Peacock streaming service was falling behind the competition. The publication reported that despite adding more than 20 million subscribers in the last quarter, Peacock lost about $1 billion.
